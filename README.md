# THE TALKING DRUM · TTD

> A sphere of **UD0** — the ROOT0 universe / biosphere. Domain: **HERMES**.

THE TALKING DRUM (TTD) — the Kele/Lokele drum language of the Congo, decoded by Carrington and the piece that opens Gleick's history of information: TWO drums, two tones — low ('male') and high ('female') — one beat per syllable, keeping only a sentence's TONE-CONTOUR and throwing the rest away, so it must REPEAT to be understood (~8 drum-words carry one spoken word; the drummers found Shannon's redundancy by ear, centuries early). Play the two pads, hear the COLLISION (~130 Kele words share '– –') and the REPAIR (a fixed stock phrase that kills the ambiguity). ⚑ Folded into the communication layer because it is BINARY — low=0, high=1, the same digits [[pulse]]/LIMEN carries and [[poetical-science]] weaves; every tapped pattern reads out as a binary number, the 'mathematical poetry in number' Ada Lovelace meant, spoken in two tones for centuries. Two-layer HONEST: the collision set, the stock phrases, and the redundancy ratio are real (Carrington via Gleick); the tone-by-tone melody of full phrases isn't transcribed in the sources, so longer contours show the LENGTH of the redundancy, not a note-perfect recording; the drum voice is synthesised. A living tradition now nearly silent — telephones finished what roads began.
